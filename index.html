📌 Millimetre (mm)

The TINIEST unit we use! Teeny tiny!

🐛 Milo says:
A millimetre is about the thickness of a fingernail! Can you see it on your ruler? Count the tiny lines between each centimetre — there are 10 mm in 1 cm!
💊
A tablet
~5 mm thick
🪙
A 1p coin
~2 mm thick
✏️
Pencil lead
~2 mm wide
🐜
An ant
~1-2 mm long
💧
Raindrop
~1-3 mm wide
🍃
Leaf vein
~1 mm wide
🔢
10 mm = 1 cm
Ten little millimetres make one centimetre!

📏 Centimetre (cm)

Small but handy — perfect for everyday measuring!

🐛 Milo says:
Spread your fingers — from your thumb tip to little finger is about 15-18 cm! We use centimetres to measure things we can hold.
How big is 1 cm?
👆 About the width of your fingernail!
📎

🔢
100 cm = 1 m
One hundred centimetres fill up a whole metre!

🚪 Metre (m)

For bigger things — doors, rooms & swimming pools!

🐛 Milo says:
Stand up and stretch your arms wide — that's about 1 metre! Most adult people are between 1.5 m and 2 m tall!

🚗 Kilometre (km)

HUGE distances — roads, cities & journeys!

🐛 Milo says:
If you walked around a running track 2.5 times, that's about 1 kilometre! We use km to measure how far places are from each other.
📍 Journey Distances:
~0.3 km — Walk to school
